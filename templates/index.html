The objective of the Air Fare Prediction project is to build a model capable of accurately forecasting airline ticket prices. This project leverages data analysis, machine learning, and statistical modeling techniques to examine past flight data and predict future airfare trends.


The Air Fare Prediction project is designed to assist travelers in better planning their trips by offering insights into potential flight prices. The model estimates airfare based on various factors such as the number of stops, travel day, travel month, airline, departure location, and destination. It has been trained using a Random Forest Regressor and further optimized through hyperparameter tuning. Once trained, the model is saved and deployed via a Flask application on a local server.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What information is needed to operate the system?
To produce forecasts, the system needs information such as the airline name, departure date, number of stops, source, and destination.
2. Is it possible for the system to forecast prices in real time?
At the moment, the system makes forecasts based on previous data. It can be expanded to operate with real-time airline data, though.
3. How precise are the forecasts?
The model's performance and the quality of the data determine the accuracy. Based on past data, the Random Forest Regressor offers a high accuracy rate.
